Frequently Asked Questions

How is this different from just using ChatGPT with custom instructions?
ChatGPT is one brain doing everything. COREX is 7 specialized agents that coordinate with each other, remember your business context across sessions, and proactively brief you. The difference is the same as between a freelancer who "does everything" and an actual team with defined roles. Also - ChatGPT doesn't send you a Telegram brief at 8 AM or route your marketing question to a marketing specialist automatically.
Is 500 tasks/month actually enough?
For most solopreneurs and small teams, yes. That's roughly 16-17 tasks per day. Each "task" is a complete interaction - asking Valencia to draft a campaign, having Sterling analyze your revenue, getting Atlas to review a technical decision. If you're running a larger operation and need more, the Unlimited tier removes the cap entirely.
What happens when I hit the 500 task limit?
Your access continues - you just can't assign new tasks until the next billing cycle or until you upgrade to Unlimited. Nothing breaks. Nothing gets deleted. Your agents and their memory stay intact.
I've bought AI tools before and stopped using them after a week. Why would this be different?
Because COREX gets better the more you use it. Most AI tools are stateless - every session starts from zero. COREX remembers your business, your preferences, and your history. Day 30 is dramatically more useful than Day 1. The morning Telegram brief alone creates a habit loop that keeps you engaged daily without effort.
Do I need to be technical to use this?
No. The entire point is that Atlas (CTO) handles technical decisions for you. You describe what you need in plain English, and the right agent handles it. The dashboard is intentionally simple - if you can write a text message, you can use COREX.

Your data stays in your workspace. Agents only access your business context - they don't share it with other users. The persistent memory is scoped to your account. We don't sell data or use it to train models. Standard enterprise-grade security practices.
